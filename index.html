Window Cleaning Basics

Clean windows improve curb appeal, let in more natural light, and help you spot potential problems like seal failures or frame damage. Here's what you should know about keeping your windows clean.

Important Things to Know

üíß How Often to Clean

Residential: Most homes benefit from professional cleaning 2-4 times per year. Dusty desert conditions may require more frequent cleaning. Commercial: Storefronts typically need monthly or bi-weekly service to maintain appearance.

üè† Inside vs. Outside

Exterior windows collect dust, pollen, and hard water spots much faster than interiors. You may need exterior cleaning more frequently than interior. Interior windows mainly need cleaning for fingerprints and dust.

ü™ü Screens Matter

Dirty screens block airflow and cast shadows on clean windows. We recommend screen cleaning at least once a year, typically in spring before you open windows for fresh air.

When to Call for Professional Cleaning

Before Selling Your Home

Clean windows significantly improve showing appearance and help buyers see rooms as brighter and more inviting.

Spring Cleaning Time

After winter dust and before you open windows for fresh air. Good time to clean screens too.

After Construction or Remodeling

Construction leaves film, paint overspray, and debris that requires professional removal.

Hard Water Buildup

If you notice cloudy spots that won't wipe away, you need professional hard water treatment before etching becomes permanent.

High or Hard-to-Reach Windows

Two-story homes, skylights, and cathedral windows require proper equipment and safety training.

Regular Maintenance

Set up quarterly or bi-annual service to keep windows consistently clean without thinking about it.
